How it works

Three steps between you and your real number.

STEP 1

Connect once

Authorize Ripster through eBay's official OAuth — read-only, sixty seconds, no password shared. Your full transaction history syncs automatically and stays current.

STEP 2

Cards pair themselves

Every purchase gets a Card ID. When the matching sale appears — any platform, any year — Ripster links the legs and computes true cost basis, fees included.

STEP 3

Export to your CPA

Schedule D and Form 8949 output with dates, basis, proceeds, and holding periods per card. The 1099-K gap, documented and defensible.

Questions

Asked by every reseller we talk to.

Can Ripster see my eBay password or change my listings?

No. The connection runs through eBay's official OAuth — you sign in on eBay's own page and grant read-only access to transaction history. Ripster never sees your password and can't touch listings, bids, or account settings.

I bought raw and sold graded a year later. Does pairing still work?

That's exactly the case the Card ID system was built for. The buy leg and sell leg can be on different platforms, years apart, raw versus slabbed — Ripster links them and carries grading fees into the cost basis.

My 1099-K shows way more than I actually made. Does Ripster fix that?

The 1099-K reports gross sales, not profit. Ripster documents the gap — fees, shipping, and cost basis — and produces the per-transaction record that supports the real number on your return.

Is Ripster a tax advisor?

No. Ripster organizes your transaction data and produces exports for you and your CPA. It doesn't give tax advice — bring the output to a qualified professional when you file.

What about my non-card sales on the same accounts?

Ripster filters obvious non-card transactions automatically and routes anything ambiguous to a review queue, so your P&L reflects cards — not the lawnmower you sold in July.
